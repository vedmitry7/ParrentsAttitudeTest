High scores on the "symbiosis" scale - 6-7 points - are sufficient to conclude that this adult does not establish a psychological distance between himself and the child, tries always to be closer to him, to satisfy his basic reasonable needs, to protect from troubles.

    Low scores on the same scale - 1-2 points - are a sign that the adult, by contrast, establishes a significant psychological distance between himself and the child, takes little care of him. It is unlikely that such an adult can be a good teacher and educator for a child.
