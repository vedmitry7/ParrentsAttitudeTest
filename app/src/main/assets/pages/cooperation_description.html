High scores on the scale of "cooperation" - 6-7 points - are a sign that the adult shows a sincere interest in what interests the child, appreciates the child's abilities, encourages the independence and initiative of the child, tries to be on equal terms with him.

Low scores on this scale - 1-2 points - indicate that the adult in relation to the child behaves in the opposite way and can not pretend to be a good teacher.
